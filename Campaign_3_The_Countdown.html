Milestone 01
Recognize & Affirm Aging as a Natural Process
Geroscience should recognize that aging is a natural process, not a disease. It should not contribute to ageism but should instead increase opportunities for people to live the way they want as they age.
CAUTION
Milestone 02
Increase Healthspan
The primary intention of geroscience is to increase healthspan — the years of life that a person is in good health — rather than just the total years of life. Potential increases in lifespan are a welcome secondary gain.
CAUTION
Milestone 03
Ensure Access & Equity
Geroscience should help address the profound question of health disparities. Who will have access to and benefit from the emerging medical innovations of geroscience, and who will be left behind? Scientific advances must be inclusive and beneficial for all and must decrease rather than increase disparities.
NOT READY
Milestone 04
Ensure Affordability for Individuals & the System
The costs of geroscience interventions should not be burdensome to consumers or limit access to care. Sufficient financial assistance should be available to ensure affordability for all. These interventions, if meaningful and accessible, should produce cost savings, or at least a good cost-benefit proposition, and improve the financial sustainability of health care systems.
NOT READY
Milestone 05
Ensure Transparency
Consumer voices must be elevated so the needs and concerns of all populations are taken into consideration when determining what constitutes a clinically relevant outcome. Policymaking must be transparent and engage the public to ensure that the outcomes represent meaningful changes in people's lives.
NOT READY
Milestone 06
Utilize an Integrated Approach
Achievements in geroscience must be fully integrated in, and remain part of, larger country-level efforts to address social determinants of health and longevity.
NOT READY

Design Notes

1
Vertical countdown timeline — Milestones descend from 2025 toward 2050. Each principle is a checkpoint on the path forward. The format creates momentum: the reader sees a clear timeline and a clear set of actions to take.
2
Progress bars with status labels — every principle gets a visual readiness indicator. RED = Not Ready, YELLOW = Caution. This is the core new component type — a gauge that says "we're not on track." Nothing in Campaign 1 or 2 uses progress bars.
3
Full article text on every principle — no shortcuts. Each milestone carries the complete principle description from the source article.

Design Notes

1
Countdown timer hero — Giant "2050" countdown display. The reader immediately understands: 2050 is the deadline, and the principles are the milestones to hit along the way.
2
Global Readiness bar — A persistent progress bar at 18% showing overall readiness. New component type — creates a single clear metric that summarizes where the world stands on geroscience readiness.
3
Horizontal status cards with gauges — Each principle is a left-bordered card with numbered badge, red status dot, full article text, and a horizontal gauge. The stacked vertical format (not a 2-column grid like C2) lets each principle breathe with its complete text.
4
Full article text on every principle — no shortcuts. Each card carries the complete principle description from the source article.
